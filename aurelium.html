The DeFi Collateral Dilemma

DeFi protocols are built on a foundation of collateral. Yet, the most common collateral assets suffer from two critical flaws:

  • Volatility Risk: Assets like ETH and BTC are powerful, but their price swings create constant liquidation risk for borrowers.
  • Centralization Risk: Fiat-backed stablecoins reintroduce the very centralization DeFi aims to escape, making them vulnerable to censorship and de-pegging events.

The Solution: Aurelium (AUL )

Aurelium (AUL) is a new primitive for DeFi: a synthetic treasury asset designed for maximum stability and reliability. Pegged to the value of gold, AUL serves as a superior form of collateral.

It combines the time-tested stability of gold with the transparent, programmable nature of the blockchain, offering a censorship-resistant asset ready to secure the next generation of financial protocols.

The Aurelium Mechanism

A protocol built on three pillars of stability and trust.

Genesis Issuance

The entire supply of Aurelium is held by the DAO treasury from genesis. This ensures a predictable, finite supply, free from the risks of centralized issuers or inflationary minting mechanisms.

Value Peg & Stability

The value of AUL is anchored to gold through DAO-managed liquidity pools and a transparent price oracle. A dedicated redemption mechanism ensures a hard price floor, managed by the protocol's treasury.

Capital-Efficient Collateral

The primary utility of AUL is to serve as high-quality collateral. By partnering with leading lending protocols, the Aurelium DAO deploys its treasury to provide deep liquidity and enable capital-efficient borrowing for the entire ecosystem.
